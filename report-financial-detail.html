Pest Control — proposal & context

Proposal — split Pest Control into its own budget line

Pest control spend currently has no home of its own — it lands in general R&M with no per-property work order, which is exactly how one intensive treatment month (this one) silently blows an entire property’s R&M budget without tripping any alert. We’d propose a fifth division alongside Grounds, R&M, Turns, and Capex Turns: Pest Control, with its own budget and its own line in this reconciliation, so a heavy month reads as its own variance instead of an unexplained R&M overage.

Context — why June was pest-heavy in the first place

Over the course of June it became clear Point Pest’s “monthly inspections” weren’t actually resulting in treatment — visits were logged, but nothing changed, while residents kept filing infestation melds. Point Pest was cancelled and Eden Advanced Pest Technologies took over the affected properties (kn47, rl21, w117, c313, ps25, ps91). June was the heaviest month by design — initial inspections and first treatments landing across several properties at once — and both treatment volume and reported infestations are trending down since.
